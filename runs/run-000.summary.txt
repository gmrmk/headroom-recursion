## 2. ESTABLISHED

1. **[KNOWN] Baker–Gill–Solovay relativization barrier (1975).** There exist oracles A, B with P^A = NP^A and P^B ≠ NP^B. Hence any technique that relativizes (survives giving every machine consistent access to an arbitrary oracle) cannot resolve P vs NP: one uniform relativizing argument would have to yield both P^A = NP^A and P^B ≠ NP^B, a contradiction.
   - *Citation:* Baker, Theodore; Gill, John; Solovay, Robert (1975). Relativizations of the P=?NP question. *SIAM J. Computing* 4.

2. **[KNOWN] Aaronson–Wigderson algebrization barrier (2008).** Algebrization generalizes relativization: an oracle is replaced by a low-degree extension Ã of it over a finite field, and a technique "algebrizes" if its steps go through relative to such Ã. Aaronson–Wigderson exhibit oracle settings showing that algebrizing techniques cannot separate P from NP. The intended direction is that the algebrization framework catches more techniques than relativization — e.g., IP = PSPACE algebrizes but does not relativize. Consequence: arithmetization-based techniques alone are insufficient for P vs NP. (Watch-item, not asserted: Impagliazzo–Kabanets–Kolokolova (2009) note algebrization is not fully robust/transitive, so "every relativizing proof algebrizes" should not be sharpened into a clean theorem without a source.)
   - *Citation:* Aaronson, Scott; Wigderson, Avi (2008). Algebrization: a new barrier in complexity theory. *STOC*.

3. **[KNOWN] Kannan fixed-polynomial lower bound (1982).** For every constant k there is a language L ∈ Σ₂ᵖ ∩ Π₂ᵖ with L ∉ SIZE(n^k). Retained as background on the landscape of unconditional circuit lower bounds; not on the current attack line.
   - *Citation:* Kannan, Ravi (1982). Circuit-size lower bounds and non-reducibility to sparse sets. *Information and Control* 55.

4. **[KNOWN] Karp–Lipton conditional collapse (1980).** If NP ⊆ P/poly, then PH ⊆ Σ₂ᵖ (the polynomial hierarchy collapses to its second level). Retained as background; the standard route to conditional separations, not on the current attack line.
   - *Citation:* Karp, Richard; Lipton, Richard (1980). Some connections between nonuniform and uniform complexity classes. *STOC*.

5. **[KNOWN] Williams' algorithmic method — the general connection (2010/2013).** Let C be a "reasonable" circuit class (closed under composition with AC⁰ and admitting the standard easy-witness machinery). Suppose that for **some superpolynomial size bound s(n) = n^{ω(1)}**, the satisfiability problem for C-circuits on n inputs of size at most s(n) can be solved in time O(2^n / n^{ω(1)}) — i.e., only barely faster than exhaustive search. Then NEXP ⊄ C. The proof is an indirect diagonalization: assuming NEXP ⊆ C, the easy-witness lemma forces succinct C-circuit witnesses whose verification circuits have *unbounded-polynomial* (hence superpolynomial-when-composed) size; the nontrivial SAT algorithm applied at that size then yields a too-efficient nondeterministic simulation, contradicting the nondeterministic time hierarchy theorem. The essential point for correctness is that the SAT algorithm must beat brute force on circuits of size larger than any single fixed polynomial — a fixed-polynomial (n^k, k constant) SAT speedup is *not* sufficient; Williams' ACC⁰ instantiation in fact tolerates size up to 2^{n^{o(1)}}. This is the reusable engine; the specific lower bound is obtained by supplying such a SAT algorithm for a specific C.
   - *Citation:* Williams, Ryan (2010). Improving exhaustive search implies superpolynomial lower bounds. *STOC*; journal version *SIAM J. Computing* 42(3), 2013.

6. **[KNOWN] Beigel–Tarui representation theorem (1994).** Every depth-d, size-s ACC⁰ circuit (AND/OR/NOT and MOD_m gates, m a fixed constant) is equivalent to a single SYM⁺ gate (an arbitrary symmetric Boolean function) applied to at most 2^{(log s)^{O(d)}} AND gates each of fan-in (log s)^{O(d)}. Proof idea (cited, not rederived): replace each subcircuit by a low-degree probabilistic polynomial over an appropriate ring (a CRT-based generalization of the Razborov–Smolensky/Toda polynomial method to MOD_m), then collapse the resulting bounded-degree polynomial into the SYM⁺∘AND normal form. For s = poly(n) and constant d the representation has size 2^{polylog(n)}, i.e., subexponential in n. This is the structural ingredient that makes ACC⁰-SAT tractable faster than brute force. **Note the top gate is *symmetric*** — this symmetry is exactly what makes the induced search tractable.
   - *Citation:* Beigel, Richard; Tarui, Jun (1994). On ACC. *Computational Complexity* 4(4), 350–366.

7. **[KNOWN] Williams' NEXP ⊄ ACC⁰ (2011/2014).** NEXP is not contained in non-uniform ACC⁰. Proof structure: apply Item 6 to reduce ACC⁰-circuit satisfiability to searching over the SYM⁺∘AND normal form, yielding an ACC⁰-SAT algorithm running in time 2^{n - n^{Ω(1)}} = O(2^n/n^{ω(1)}) on circuits of the required superpolynomial size; then invoke Item 5 with C = ACC⁰ to conclude NEXP ⊄ ACC⁰. Thus the result is exactly the algorithmic-method engine (Item 5) instantiated by the structure theorem (Item 6).
   - *Citation:* Williams, Ryan (2011). Non-uniform ACC circuit lower bounds. *CCC*; journal version *J. ACM* 61(1), 2014.

## 3. FRONTIER

**Target result:** a nontrivial satisfiability algorithm — running in time O(2^n / n^{ω(1)}) on circuits of some superpolynomial size s(n) = n^{ω(1)} (the regime Item 5 actually requires) — for depth-2 linear-threshold circuits (THR∘THR, i.e. TC⁰₂). By Item 5 this yields **NEXP ⊄ TC⁰₂**. This is the natural next target of the paradigm, **on the direct path to NEXP ⊄ TC⁰ via the inclusion TC⁰₂ ⊆ TC⁰** (which makes NEXP ⊄ TC⁰₂ a *weaker/easier* consequence of NEXP ⊄ TC⁰). It is **incomparable to — not above — the ACC⁰ result of Item 7**: neither ACC⁰ ⊆ TC⁰₂ nor TC⁰₂ ⊆ ACC⁰ is established (the former would need a poly-size depth-2 threshold simulation of ACC⁰, which Beigel–Tarui does not give — it yields only a quasipoly-size *symmetric* top gate; the latter would put MAJ ∈ ACC⁰, the standard conjecture believed false). Its exact open-vs-solved status should be confirmed against a source before being asserted.

(a) **Why it suffices.** Item 5 is class-generic: it converts any such SAT algorithm into the lower bound with no further ingredients, provided C meets Item 5's hypotheses. Caveat to flag as a boundary condition: Item 5 requires C closed under composition with AC⁰, and the exact-depth-2 slice TC⁰₂ is not itself AC⁰-closed. The method's clean target is therefore an AC⁰-closed threshold class (full TC⁰, or TC⁰₂ padded to the smallest AC⁰-closed class containing it); the closure status of the strict depth-2 slice is a boundary detail, not something to assume away. Modulo this, supplying the analogous algorithmic input for THR∘THR discharges the remaining obligation.

(b) **Obstacle.** The ACC⁰ SAT algorithm rests on the Beigel–Tarui collapse to a *symmetric* top gate over ANDs (SYM⁺∘AND, Item 6). The bottleneck for THR∘THR is the **top threshold gate**: a threshold TOP gate over threshold subcircuits has **no known SYM⁺-style collapse** — no established normal form reducing it to a symmetric (or otherwise SAT-tractable) top gate of subexponential size. Corroborating this, a single threshold (symmetric-function) requires probabilistic-polynomial degree Θ(√n) even for weak approximation (Paturi 1992), so the low-degree depth-reduction that drives the ACC⁰ algorithm has no established analogue here. Note further (gate topology, no retrieval needed) that TC⁰₂ = THR∘THR has a *threshold* top gate, which is not an ACC⁰ gate; hence a known ACC⁰∘THR lower bound (bottom threshold layer, ACC⁰ above) cannot silently already yield NEXP ⊄ TC⁰₂ — the target genuinely sits just beyond that route, precisely because of the top threshold gate.

(c) **Candidate strategy.** Two admissible routes, both to enter ESTABLISHED only once verified against a source: (i) **Retrieve, then extend the known partial progress.** Williams and collaborators obtained lower bounds for ACC⁰ *augmented with a thin bottom layer of threshold gates* (ACC⁰∘THR); the precise statement and its exact ceiling must be pulled from a post-2014 source (WebSearch/WebFetch currently blocked pending a user permission grant — see FAILED) before any claim about how far the method already reaches is asserted, and per (b) that route does not cover a threshold top gate. (ii) **Replace the missing normal form.** Seek a satisfiability-oriented structural handle for THR∘THR that substitutes for Item 6 and specifically tames the *top* threshold gate — e.g., a "few-linear-forms" decomposition or a correlation/derandomization argument enumerating satisfying assignments in time 2^{n-n^{Ω(1)}} without a full low-degree polynomial. Any such construction must be exhibited with an explicit runtime bound clearing O(2^n/n^{ω(1)}) at superpolynomial circuit size, and checked against the Θ(√n)-degree obstruction, before entering ESTABLISHED.
