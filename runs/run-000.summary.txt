# P vs NP Research Document — Barrier-Unification Line (audit-hardened survey)

## ATTACK LINE

Determine whether the three established barriers to separating P from NP — relativization (Baker–Gill–Solovay), algebrization (Aaronson–Wigderson), and natural proofs (Razborov–Rudich) — admit a single precise characterization of the class of proof techniques they *jointly* exclude, and identify the minimal formal property a putative separation proof must possess to lie outside all three at once. The value of this line is that "avoid the barriers" is currently a folklore checklist rather than a theorem: each barrier is proved by a different device (oracle pairs; low-degree oracle extensions; a constructive-largeness argument against pseudorandom functions), and no unified statement pins down the intersection of what they forbid. Making that intersection precise is a prerequisite for judging whether any known technique (e.g. the arithmetic/counting arguments behind fixed-polynomial lower bounds) actually escapes all three, and it is fully tool-free.

## ESTABLISHED

**1. [KNOWN] Relativization barrier (Baker, Gill, Solovay 1975).** There exist oracles A, B with P^A = NP^A and P^B ≠ NP^B. Any proof technique that stays valid when an arbitrary oracle is adjoined to every machine in the argument (a "relativizing" technique) must yield the same verdict in every oracle world; since the P vs NP question has opposite answers relative to A and to B, no relativizing technique can settle it. This excludes pure diagonalization/simulation as a complete solution.

**2. [KNOWN] Algebrization barrier (Aaronson, Wigderson 2008).** Aaronson and Wigderson exhibit oracle settings showing that techniques which remain valid when machines are given access to a low-degree polynomial extension of the oracle — "algebrizing" techniques, a class intended to capture arithmetization-based methods such as those used for IP = PSPACE and the PCP theorem — likewise cannot resolve P vs NP. (Stated here as a paraphrase of the barrier's import; the precise oracle-separation formulation is that of the cited paper.)

**3. [KNOWN] Natural proofs barrier (Razborov, Rudich 1994).** Identify an n-variable Boolean function with its truth table of length N = 2ⁿ. A sequence of properties Γₙ of such functions is a *natural proof useful against* a circuit class C if it is: (i) **constructive** — membership f ∈ Γₙ is decidable in time poly(N) given the truth table; (ii) **large** — a fraction at least 2^{−O(n)} of all n-variable functions lie in Γₙ; and (iii) **useful against C** — every function with C-circuits is excluded from Γₙ for all large n. Their theorem: if strong pseudorandom function generators exist (a standard cryptographic hardness assumption), then no natural property is useful against P/poly. Because most classical combinatorial lower-bound techniques produce properties that are constructive and large, this shows — under that assumption — such techniques cannot yield P/poly lower bounds separating NP from P/poly. *(Bibliographic year recalled from standard literature, not cross-checked against this session's snippets; the technical content above is the load-bearing part and is stated in full.)*

**4. [KNOWN] Fixed-polynomial lower bound in Σ₂ (Kannan 1982).** For every k, Σ₂ ∩ Π₂ (and hence Σ₂, since Σ₂ ∩ Π₂ ⊆ Σ₂) contains a language not computable by circuits of size n^k. This places a hard language at a fixed level of the polynomial hierarchy against a *fixed*-polynomial circuit bound; it is not a uniform separation of P from NP, and gives no lower bound against P/poly (arbitrary polynomial size).

**5. [KNOWN] Karp–Lipton collapse (Karp, Lipton 1980; with Sipser, strengthened by Yap).** If NP ⊆ P/poly then the polynomial hierarchy collapses to its second level, Σ₂. Consequently NP ⊆ P/poly cannot hold unless PH collapses, and this implication is a standard tool in results that locate hard languages in low levels of PH, such as item 4.

## FRONTIER

**Next result.** Produce a single formal statement of the form: "a proof of NP ⊄ P/poly must fail to satisfy property Φ," where Φ is a conjunction of three closure/definability conditions — one per barrier — each stated so that a technique satisfying it is provably blocked by the corresponding theorem of items 1–3. Concretely: (Φ₁) invariance under oracle adjunction; (Φ₂) invariance under low-degree oracle extension; (Φ₃) yielding a constructive-and-large property useful against P/poly. The target is a proof that these three are logically independent (no two imply the third) together with the exact intersection Φ₁∧Φ₂∧Φ₃ that a separation must avoid.

**(a) Why it suffices.** A precise Φ converts the informal "beat all three barriers" test into a checkable predicate on proof techniques. It would let one *verify*, rather than assert, that a candidate method (e.g. Kannan-style counting, or Williams-style algebraic-plus-nonuniform arguments) lies outside all three — which is the concrete deliverable the attack line is organized around, and the first step past a survey of separately-proved barrier theorems.

**(b) Obstacle.** The three barriers are proved in incompatible formal frameworks: relativization and algebrization quantify over oracle worlds, while natural proofs quantify over the syntactic form of the property a technique constructs. There is no agreed single meta-model of "proof technique" in which all three closure conditions are simultaneously expressible, so Φ₁, Φ₂, Φ₃ currently live in different type universes and cannot be conjoined as stated without first fixing such a model.

**(c) Candidate strategy.** Adopt a proof-as-property meta-model (a proof of a circuit lower bound is identified with the sequence of hard-set predicates it certifies), and re-express each barrier as a closure condition on that object: Φ₁, Φ₂ as invariance of the certified predicate under oracle/low-degree-extension relativization of the underlying machine model, Φ₃ as the Razborov–Rudich constructive-largeness condition. Then test independence by exhibiting, for each pair, a technique satisfying two conditions but not the third — the natural-proofs literature already supplies candidate witnesses (relativizing-and-algebrizing arguments that are non-constructive, and constructive arguments that are non-relativizing), which would be the first concrete claims to attempt.
