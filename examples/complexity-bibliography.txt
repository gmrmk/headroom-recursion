Cook, Stephen (1971). The complexity of theorem-proving procedures. STOC. Defines NP-completeness; SAT is NP-complete.
Levin, Leonid (1973). Universal sequential search problems. Independent discovery of NP-completeness.
Karp, Richard (1972). Reducibility among combinatorial problems. 21 NP-complete problems.
Karp, Richard and Lipton, Richard (1980). Some connections between nonuniform and uniform complexity classes. STOC 302-309. NP in P/poly implies PH collapses to Sigma2 (with Sipser; strengthened by Yap).
Yap, Chee K. (1983). Some consequences of non-uniform conditions on uniform classes. Theoretical Computer Science / Information and Control. Sharpens the Karp-Lipton collapse to Sigma2.
Kannan, Ravi (1982). Circuit-size lower bounds and non-reducibility to sparse sets. Information and Control 55. For every k, Sigma2 ∩ Pi2 (and Sigma2) contains a language not in SIZE(n^k) — the fixed-polynomial lower bound.
Baker, Theodore, Gill, John, Solovay, Robert (1975). Relativizations of the P=?NP question. SIAM J. Computing 4. Oracles A, B with P^A = NP^A and P^B != NP^B; relativizing techniques cannot resolve P vs NP.
Razborov, Alexander and Rudich, Steven (1994/1997). Natural proofs. STOC 1994 / JCSS 55. Constructive large properties useful against P/poly break pseudorandom generators — the natural proofs barrier.
Aaronson, Scott and Wigderson, Avi (2008). Algebrization: a new barrier in complexity theory. STOC. Arithmetization-based techniques also cannot resolve P vs NP.
Williams, Ryan (2010/2011). Non-uniform ACC circuit lower bounds. CCC/JACM. NEXP not in ACC0 via satisfiability algorithms implying lower bounds — evades all three barriers.
Williams, Ryan (2013). Improving exhaustive search implies superpolynomial lower bounds. SIAM J. Computing. The algorithmic method: faster circuit-SAT gives circuit lower bounds.
Murray, Cody and Williams, Ryan (2018). Circuit lower bounds for nondeterministic quasi-polytime. STOC. NQP not in ACC0 — tightens the algorithmic method.
Hastad, Johan (1986). Almost optimal lower bounds for small depth circuits. STOC. Parity requires exponential-size AC0 circuits (switching lemma).
Razborov, Alexander (1985). Lower bounds on the monotone complexity of some Boolean functions. Clique requires superpolynomial monotone circuits.
Smolensky, Roman (1987). Algebraic methods in the theory of lower bounds for Boolean circuit complexity. AC0[p] lower bounds via polynomial approximation.
Toda, Seinosuke (1991). PP is as hard as the polynomial-time hierarchy. SIAM J. Computing. PH is in P^PP.
Impagliazzo, Russell and Wigderson, Avi (1997). P = BPP if E requires exponential circuits. Derandomization from hardness.
Kabanets, Valentine and Impagliazzo, Russell (2004). Derandomizing polynomial identity testing means proving circuit lower bounds. Computational Complexity 13.
Oliveira, Igor and Pich, Jan and Santhanam, Rahul (2019+). Hardness magnification near state-of-the-art lower bounds. Weak lower bounds for compression problems magnify to major separations.
Chen, Lijie and Jin, Ce and Williams, Ryan (2020). Hardness magnification for all sparse NP languages. FOCS.
Buhrman, Harry and Homer, Steven (1992). Superpolynomial circuits, almost sparse oracles and the exponential hierarchy. Fixed-polynomial lower bounds for larger classes.
Santhanam, Rahul (2009). Circuit lower bounds for Merlin-Arthur classes. SIAM J. Computing. MA/1 not in SIZE(n^k) for every k.
Vinodchandran, N. V. (2005). A note on the circuit complexity of PP. PP not in SIZE(n^k) for every fixed k.
Arora, Sanjeev and Barak, Boaz (2009). Computational Complexity: A Modern Approach. Cambridge University Press. Standard reference text.
Deolalikar, Vinay (2010). Claimed proof of P != NP via descriptive complexity / statistical physics; withdrawn after the finite-model-theory and solution-space arguments were shown flawed.
